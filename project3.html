Retail Sales Performance Analysis Using SQL

Tool: PostgreSQL | Domain: Retail Sales & Customer Insights

Project Overview

This project explores how SQL can be used to analyze retail performance data using PostgreSQL. It covers data modeling, relational joins, aggregation, and subquery techniques to uncover key insights about customers, regions, and product sales. The dataset contains customer, product, and sales_order tables.

Database Schema

The relational model consists of three core tables:

  • customer ‚Äì Customer demographics and segmentation info.
  • product ‚Äì Product categories and subcategories.
  • sales_order ‚Äì Transactional data with sales, discount, and dates.

Business Objectives

  • Understand sales distribution by region and customer segment.
  • Identify top-performing customers and regions by sales volume.
  • Determine customer purchase behavior across time and segments.
  • Use subqueries to compare customer demographics against overall averages.

SQL Techniques Used

  • Joins: INNER JOIN, LEFT JOIN, RIGHT JOIN, FULL JOIN to merge datasets.
  • Aggregation: SUM, COUNT, MAX for performance metrics.
  • Filtering: WHERE, BETWEEN, and GROUP BY with HAVING for advanced queries.
  • Subqueries: Nested queries to compare customer ages and regional performance.

Key SQL Queries & Insights

Below are some of the important queries and their insights.

SELECT region, SUM(sales) AS total_sales
FROM customer
INNER JOIN sales_order
ON customer.customer_id = sales_order.customer_id
GROUP BY region
ORDER BY total_sales DESC;

Insight: Revealed the top-performing regions based on total sales volume.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Top Customers by Total Sales

SELECT customer_name, SUM(sales) AS total_sales
FROM customer
INNER JOIN sales_order
ON customer.customer_id = sales_order.customer_id
GROUP BY customer_name
ORDER BY total_sales DESC;

Insight: Identified high-value customers contributing the most to total revenue.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Orders by Segment in 2017

SELECT segment, COUNT(order_date) AS total_orders
FROM customer
INNER JOIN sales_order
ON customer.customer_id = sales_order.customer_id
WHERE order_date BETWEEN '2017-01-01' AND '2017-12-31'
GROUP BY segment
ORDER BY total_orders DESC;

Insight: Helped determine which segments were most active within the year.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Customers Older Than Average

SELECT customer_name, age
FROM customer
WHERE age > (SELECT AVG(age) FROM customer);

Insight: Compared customers‚Äô age distribution against the overall average using a subquery.
